## Why "removed" aesthetics still appeared

Two separate sources for aesthetics:

| Path | Source |
|---|---|
| Live web app | Supabase first; local `_A` array as offline fallback |
| Batch test (CSVs) | **Always** local `_A` — never touches Supabase |

So even if Sameer deleted `colorpop` from Supabase before, the batch test scripts read from the bundled local `_A` and the prompts still included it. V5.5 syncs both: removes from local `_A`, plus a Supabase migration script.

## V5.5 changes

### 1. Aesthetics removed entirely from `_A`

- **Scandi Color-Pop (`colorpop`)** — fundamentally a color-block-wall aesthetic, incompatible with brand standard
- **Sunset Bedtime (`sunset`)** — cloud nightlight per Sameer's call

Pool: V5.4 14 → V5.5 12

### 2. Aesthetic data cleaned

- **Coastal `materials`** — stripped "rattan pendant" (was producing pendants directly above products in Bouncer/Crib/Comforter), added explicit anti-directive

### 3. New directive — Centering priority

Added to all Shot 1, after CROP SAFETY:

```
CENTERING — PRIORITY OVER COMPOSITIONAL BALANCE
If accent props (baskets, books, plants, lamps, stools) are placed in the
scene, they go on BOTH sides of the product or behind it — they do NOT
push the product off-center to balance the composition. The product stays
centered even if that means accent props sit asymmetrically. Centering the
product overrides classical rule-of-thirds composition.
```

Addresses: AI was using rule-of-thirds composition when one side had props (window, books, basket) — pushing product to the opposite side. Now centering is explicitly higher-priority than balance.

### 4. New directive — Architecture (no arches anywhere)

Added to all Shot 1, after CROP SAFETY (was previously mechanism-only):

```
ARCHITECTURE — NO ARCHES OR ALCOVES
The room has flat rectangular walls, standard square doorways, and a flat
ceiling. NO arched alcoves, NO arched doorways, NO arched niches, NO curved
architectural openings, NO vaulted or coved ceilings, NO recessed wall
arches behind the product. Architecture is plain, modern, and rectangular
throughout the entire frame.
```

Addresses: V4 era had per-shot arch quarantine ("Use the EXACT same wall, floor… Do NOT introduce new architectural features, arches, nooks, or corridors"). When Shot 7 (Architectural Context) was killed in V5.2, that quarantine logic went with it. V5.5 restores it as a global rule on every shot.

Removes redundant `ROOM GEOMETRY — STANDARD RECTANGULAR` block from mechanism shots (now covered globally).

### 5. New directive — Minimum styling for mechanism shots

Added to mechanism Shot 1:

```
MINIMUM STYLING — NOT EMPTY
Include 2-3 small accent items appropriate to the aesthetic to give the
scene a real-home feel: a soft woven basket, a folded knit throw, a small
soft toy, a stack of 2-3 board books, a small wooden stool, or a single
low plant in a ceramic pot. The room should feel lived-in, NOT bare or
empty. Accent items are small and supporting — they do NOT include any
secondary hero-scale furniture.
```

Addresses Play Gym empty shot.

### 6. Negative prompt expanded

Added: NO arched doorways or arched niches in the background, NO curved architectural openings, NO vaulted or coved ceilings, NO rattan or woven pendant lights hanging in the frame above the product.

## Deployment — TWO STEPS for the live app

### Step 1: Run the V5.5 SQL in Supabase

Open `V5.5_supabase_aesthetics_update.sql`. **Run the inspection SELECT first** to confirm what's currently in your `aesthetics_t1` table. Then run the DELETE/UPDATE statements. Final verify SELECT should show 12 rows.

Replaces the live prompter logic. Once both steps are done, the live app and batch testing both run V5.5 cleanly.
